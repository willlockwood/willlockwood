\nPER CURIAM
\nMINISTRY OF DEFENSE AND SUPPORT FOR ARMEDFORCES OF ISLAMIC REPUBLIC OF IRAN V. ELAHI
\n546 U. S. ____ (2006)
\nSUPREME COURT OF THE UNITED STATES
\n
\n

MINISTRY OF DEFENSE AND SUPPORT FOR THE\nARMED FORCES OF THE ISLAMIC REPUBLIC\nOF IRAN v. DARIUSH ELAHI

\n

on petition for writ of certiorari to the united states court of appeals for the ninth circuit

\n

No. 04\xe2\x80\x931095.\xe2\x80\x83Decided February 21, 2006

\n

\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0Per Curiam.

\n

\n\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0A private citizen seeks to attach an asset belonging to Iran\xe2\x80\x99s Ministry of Defense in order to help satisfy a judgment for money damages. The question raised is whether the Foreign Sovereign Immunities Act of 1976 (FSIA or Act), 28 U. S.\xc2\xa0C. \xc2\xa71602 et seq. (2000 ed. and Supp. III), forbids that attachment.

\n

\n\n\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0The judgment for money damages consists of a default judgment against the Islamic Republic of Iran (for about $300 million) that the private citizen, Dariush Elahi, obtained in a federal-court lawsuit claiming that the Republic had murdered his brother. Elahi v. Islamic Republic of Iran, 124 F.\xc2\xa0Supp. 2d 97, 103 (DC 2000). The asset is an arbitration award (against a third party), which Iran\xe2\x80\x99s Ministry of Defense obtained in Switzerland. Ministry of Defense and Support for Armed Forces of Islamic Republic of Iran v. Cubic Defense Systems, Inc., 385 F.\xc2\xa03d 1206, 1211 (CA9 2004). The Ministry asked the Federal District Court for the Southern District of California to confirm the award. Ministry of Defense and Support for Armed Forces of Islamic Republic of Iran v. Cubic Defense Systems, Inc., 236 F.\xc2\xa0Supp. 2d 1140 (2002). The court did so. And Elahi then intervened, seeking to impose a lien upon the award. The Ministry opposed the attachment on the ground that the Act grants it immunity from such a claim.

\n

\n\n\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0The Federal District Court rejected the Ministry\xe2\x80\x99s immunity defense on the ground that, by suing to enforce the award, the Ministry had waived any such immunity. On appeal the Ninth Circuit disagreed with the District Court about waiver. But it then found against the Ministry on a different ground\xe2\x80\x94a ground that the parties had not argued. The Act says that under certain conditions the property of an \xe2\x80\x9cagency or instrumentality\xe2\x80\x9d of a foreign government is \xe2\x80\x9cnot \xe2\x80\xa6 immune from attachment\xe2\x80\x9d if the agency is \xe2\x80\x9cengaged in commercial activity in the United States.\xe2\x80\x9d 28 U. S.\xc2\xa0C. \xc2\xa71610(b) (emphasis added). The Court of Appeals found that the Ministry engages in commercial activity and that the other conditions were satisfied. 385 F.\xc2\xa03d, at 1219\xe2\x80\x931222 (applying \xc2\xa71610(b)(2)). And it held that this section of the Act barred the Ministry\xe2\x80\x99s assertion of immunity. Ibid.

\n

\n\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0The Ministry filed a petition for certiorari asking us to review that decision. The Solicitor General agrees with the Ministry that we should grant the writ but limited to the Ministry\xe2\x80\x99s Question 1, namely whether \xe2\x80\x9cthe property of a foreign state stricto sensu, situated in the United States\xe2\x80\x9d is \xe2\x80\x9cimmune from attachment \xe2\x80\xa6 as provided in the Foreign Sovereign Immunities Act.\xe2\x80\x9d Pet. for Cert. i (citing \xc2\xa7\xc2\xa71603(a), 1610(a)). The Solicitor General also asks us to vacate the judgment of the Court of Appeals and remand the case for consideration of whether the Ministry is simply a \xe2\x80\x9cforeign state\xe2\x80\x9d (what the Ministry calls \xe2\x80\x9ca foreign state stricto sensu\xe2\x80\x9d) or whether the Ministry is an \xe2\x80\x9cagency or instrumentality\xe2\x80\x9d of a foreign state (as the Ninth Circuit held). Brief for United States as Amicus Curiae 15\xe2\x80\x9317. We grant the writ limited to Question 1.

\n

\n\n\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0The Act, as it applies to the \xe2\x80\x9cproperty in the United States of a foreign state,\xe2\x80\x9d \xc2\xa71610(a) (emphasis added), does not contain the \xe2\x80\x9cengaged in commercial activity\xe2\x80\x9d exception that the Ninth Circuit described. That exception applies only where the property at issue is property of an \xe2\x80\x9cagency or instrumentality\xe2\x80\x9d of a foreign state. Compare \xc2\xa71610(b) (\xe2\x80\x9cproperty \xe2\x80\xa6 of an agency or instrumentality of a foreign state engaged in commercial activity\xe2\x80\x9d) with \xc2\xa71610(a) (\xe2\x80\x9cproperty \xe2\x80\xa6 of a foreign state used for a commercial activity\xe2\x80\x9d) (emphasis added). The difference is critical. Moreover, in the Solicitor General\xe2\x80\x99s view a defense ministry (unlike, say, a government-owned commercial enterprise) generally is not an \xe2\x80\x9cagency or instrumentality\xe2\x80\x9d of a foreign state but an inseparable part of the state itself. Brief for United States as Amicus Curiae 8\xe2\x80\x9311; see also Transaero, Inc. v. La Fuerza Aerea Boliviana, 30 F.\xc2\xa03d 148, 153 (CADC 1994) (\xe2\x80\x9chold[ing] that armed forces are as a rule so closely bound up with the structure of the state that they must in all cases be considered as the \xe2\x80\x98foreign state\xe2\x80\x99 itself, rather than a separate \xe2\x80\x98agency or instrumentality\xe2\x80\x99 of the state\xe2\x80\x9d).

\n

\n\n\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0We shall not now determine whether the Solicitor General is correct about the status of the Ministry, for the Ninth Circuit did not address the question nor did the parties argue the matter before the Circuit. Neither can we fault the Ministry for that failure. As we said, supra, at 1, the District Court based its denial of immunity upon waiver. The parties\xe2\x80\x99 Ninth Circuit briefs focused on matters not relevant here (such as the waiver question), with one exception. The exception consists of a footnote in Elahi\xe2\x80\x99s brief mentioning the Act\xe2\x80\x99s \xe2\x80\x9cagency and instrumentality\xe2\x80\x9d provision. That footnote, however, does not ask for affirmance on that basis; nor did it provide the Ministry with clear notice that a reply was necessary. Answering Brief of Appellee in No. 03\xe2\x80\x9355015 (CA9), p. 45, n. 27 (stating that \xe2\x80\x9c[i]f [the Ministry] is considered \xe2\x80\x98an agency or instrumentality of a foreign state,\xe2\x80\x99 rather than the foreign state itself, Mr. Elahi\xe2\x80\x99s attachment still is valid\xe2\x80\x9d (emphasis added)).

\n

\n\n\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0The Ninth Circuit said that it was free to affirm on \xe2\x80\x9cany ground supported by the record.\xe2\x80\x9d 385 F.\xc2\xa03d, at 1219, n. 15. But the court did not explain what in the record might demonstrate that the Ministry is an \xe2\x80\x9cagency or instrumentality\xe2\x80\x9d of the state rather than an integral part of the state itself. The court noted that \xe2\x80\x9cElahi appears to concede\xe2\x80\x9d that the Ministry is an \xe2\x80\x9cagency and instrumentality,\xe2\x80\x9d id., at 1218, n.\xc2\xa013, but any relevant concession would have to have come from the Ministry, not from Elahi, whose position the concession favors. Thus, in implicitly concluding that the Ministry was an \xe2\x80\x9cagency or instrumentality\xe2\x80\x9d of the Republic of Iran within the meaning of \xc2\xa71610(b), the Ninth Circuit either mistakenly relied on a concession by respondent that could not possibly bind petitioner, or else erroneously presumed that there was no relevant distinction between a foreign state and its agencies or instrumentalities for purposes of that subsection. See \xc2\xa71603(a), (b). Either way, the Ninth Circuit committed error that was essential to its judgment in favor of respondent.

\n

\n\n\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0Because the Ninth Circuit did not consider, and the Ministry had no reasonable opportunity to argue, the critical legal point we have mentioned, we vacate the judgment of the Ninth Circuit, and remand the case for further proceedings consistent with this opinion.

\n

It is so ordered.
