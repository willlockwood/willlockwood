\n\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0The petition for writ of certiorari to the United States Court of Appeals for the Fifth Circuit and the motion of petitioner for leave to proceed in forma pauperis are granted. The judgment is vacated, and the case is remanded to the Fifth Circuit for further consideration.

\n

\n\n\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0The Fifth Circuit concluded that petitioner\xe2\x80\x99s prior conviction for simple possession of a controlled substance constituted a \xe2\x80\x9ccontrolled substance offense\xe2\x80\x9d for purposes of United States Sentencing Commission, Guidelines Manual \xc2\xa74B1.1(a) (2003). The term \xe2\x80\x9ccontrolled substance offense\xe2\x80\x9d is defined in pertinent part, however, as \xe2\x80\x9can offense under federal or state law . . . that prohibits . . . the possession of a controlled substance (or a counterfeit substance) with intent to manufacture, import, export, distribute, or dispense.\xe2\x80\x9d \xc2\xa74B1.2(b) (emphasis added). Accordingly, the Fifth Circuit erred in treating petitioner\xe2\x80\x99s conviction for simple possession as a \xe2\x80\x9ccontrolled substance offense.\xe2\x80\x9d The Solicitor General acknowledges that the Fifth Circuit incorrectly ruled for the United States on this ground.
