Ohio Citizens for Responsible Energy, Inc. v. Nuclear Regulatory Comm\'n, 479 U.S. 1312 (1986)

Ohio Citizens for Responsible Energy, Inc. v. Nuclear Regulatory Comm\'n

No. A-480

Decided Dec. 31, 1986

479 U.S. 1312

JUSTICE SCALIA, Circuit Justice.

\n

Ohio Citizens for Responsible Energy, Inc., has filed with me as Circuit Justice for the Sixth Circuit an "Application to Stay Mandate of United States Court of Appeals for the Sixth Circuit Pending Certiorari," seeking an order under 28 U.S.C. \xc2\xa7 2101(f) staying the full\x1epower operation of the Perry Nuclear Power Plant located near Cleveland, Ohio. The order sought would remain in effect until the Court of Appeals for the Sixth Circuit issues its final decision in the pending suit filed by the applicant against the Nuclear Regulatory Commission, and, should the applicant be unsuccessful in that suit, until disposition of a petition for writ of certiorari in this Court.

\n

The application must be denied. Section 2101(f) provides:

\n

"In any case in which the final judgment or decree of any

Page 479 U. S. 1313

court is subject to review by the Supreme Court on writ of certiorari, the execution and enforcement of such judgment or decree may be stayed for a reasonable time to enable the party aggrieved to obtain a writ of certiorari from the Supreme Court."

\n

(Emphasis added.) It is clear from this language that even though certiorari review of interlocutory orders of federal courts is available, see 28 U.S.C. \xc2\xa7\xc2\xa7 1254(1) and 1292, it is only the execution or enforcement of final orders that is stayable under \xc2\xa7 2101(f). See Twentieth Century Airlines Inc. v. Ryan, 74 S.Ct. 8, 10, 98 L.Ed. 1143, 1145 (1953) (Reed, J., in chambers). In this case, the only extant order which, if stayed, could conceivably affect the full\x1epower operation of the Perry plant is the Sixth Circuit\'s order of December 23, 1986, lifting the stay of full\x1epower operation that it imposed on November 13, 1986. That order, however -- like the stay itself -- is interlocutory.

Ohio Citizens for Responsible Energy, Inc. v. Nuclear Regulatory Comm\'n

No. A-480

Decided Dec. 31, 1986

479 U.S. 1312

JUSTICE SCALIA, Circuit Justice.

\n

Ohio Citizens for Responsible Energy, Inc., has filed with me as Circuit Justice for the Sixth Circuit an "Application to Stay Mandate of United States Court of Appeals for the Sixth Circuit Pending Certiorari," seeking an order under 28 U.S.C. \xc2\xa7 2101(f) staying the full\x1epower operation of the Perry Nuclear Power Plant located near Cleveland, Ohio. The order sought would remain in effect until the Court of Appeals for the Sixth Circuit issues its final decision in the pending suit filed by the applicant against the Nuclear Regulatory Commission, and, should the applicant be unsuccessful in that suit, until disposition of a petition for writ of certiorari in this Court.

\n

The application must be denied. Section 2101(f) provides:

\n

"In any case in which the final judgment or decree of any

Page 479 U. S. 1313

court is subject to review by the Supreme Court on writ of certiorari, the execution and enforcement of such judgment or decree may be stayed for a reasonable time to enable the party aggrieved to obtain a writ of certiorari from the Supreme Court."

\n

(Emphasis added.) It is clear from this language that even though certiorari review of interlocutory orders of federal courts is available, see 28 U.S.C. \xc2\xa7\xc2\xa7 1254(1) and 1292, it is only the execution or enforcement of final orders that is stayable under \xc2\xa7 2101(f). See Twentieth Century Airlines Inc. v. Ryan, 74 S.Ct. 8, 10, 98 L.Ed. 1143, 1145 (1953) (Reed, J., in chambers). In this case, the only extant order which, if stayed, could conceivably affect the full\x1epower operation of the Perry plant is the Sixth Circuit\'s order of December 23, 1986, lifting the stay of full\x1epower operation that it imposed on November 13, 1986. That order, however -- like the stay itself -- is interlocutory.

\n

What the applicant would require in order to achieve the substantive relief that it seeks is an original writ of injunction, pursuant to the All\x1eWrits Act, 28 U.S.C. \xc2\xa7 1651(a), and this Court\'s Rule 44.1, against full\x1epower operation of the power plant. A Circuit Justice\'s issuance of such a writ -- which, unlike a \xc2\xa7 2101(f) stay, does not simply suspend judicial alteration of the status quo but grants judicial intervention that has been withheld by lower courts -- demands a significantly higher justification than that described in the \xc2\xa7 2101(f) stay cases cited by the applicant, e.g., Rostker v. Goldberg, 448 U. S. 1306, 448 U. S. 1308 (1980) (BRENNAN, J., in chambers). The Circuit Justice\'s injunctive power is to be used "sparingly and only in the most critical and exigent circumstances,\'" Fishman v. Schaffer, 429 U.S. 1325, 1326 (MARSHALL, J., in chambers) (quoting Williams v. Rhodes, 89 S.Ct. 1, 2, 21 L.Ed.2d 69, 70 (1968) (Stewart, J., in chambers)), and only where the legal rights at issue are "indisputably clear," Communist Party of Indiana v. Whitcomb, 409 U. S. 1235 (1972) (REHNQUIST, J., in chambers). Moreover, the applicant must demonstrate that the injunctive

Page 479 U. S. 1314

relief is "necessary or appropriate in aid of [the Court\'s] jurisdictio[n]." 28 U.S.C. \xc2\xa7 1651(a). I will not consider counsel to have asked for such extraordinary relief where, as here, he has neither specifically requested it nor addressed the peculiar requirements for its issuance.\n

\n

The application for stay is denied.
