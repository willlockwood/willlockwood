\n\t\t\t\tDAN\xe2\x80\x99S CITY USED CARS, INC., dba DAN\xe2\x80\x99S CITY AUTO BODY v. PELKEY\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\tcertiorari to the supreme court of new hampshire\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\tNo. 12\xe2\x80\x9352.\xe2\x80\x83Argued March 20, 2013\xe2\x80\x94Decided May 13, 2013\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\tThe Federal Aviation Administration Authorization Act of 1994 (FAAAA) preempts state laws \xe2\x80\x9crelated to a price, route, or service of any motor carrier .\xc2\xa0.\xc2\xa0. with respect to the transportation of property.\xe2\x80\x9d \n\n49 U.\xc2\xa0S.\xc2\xa0C. \xc2\xa714501(c)(1). This provision borrows from the Airline Deregulation Act of 1978 (ADA), which preempts state laws \xe2\x80\x9crelated to a price, route, or service of an air carrier,\xe2\x80\x9d \xc2\xa741713(b)(1), but it adds the important qualification, \xe2\x80\x9cwith respect to transportation of property.\xe2\x80\x9d \n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tPlaintiff-respondent Pelkey brought suit in New Hampshire Superior Court, alleging that defendant-petitioner Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City Used Cars (Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City), a towing company, took custody of his car after towing it from his landlord\xe2\x80\x99s parking lot without Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s knowledge, failed to notify him of its plan to auction the car, held an auction despite Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s notice that he wanted to reclaim the car, and eventually traded the car away without compensating Pelkey for the loss of his vehicle. In disposing of his car, Pelkey further alleged, Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City did not meet the requirements contained in chapter 262 of the New Hampshire Revised Statutes Annotated, which regulates the disposal of abandoned vehicles by a \xe2\x80\x9cstorage company.\xe2\x80\x9d Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City\xe2\x80\x99s misconduct, Pelkey charged, both violated New Hampshire\xe2\x80\x99s Consumer Protection Act and breached the towing company\xe2\x80\x99s statutory and common-law duties as a bailee to use reasonable care while in possession of a bailor\xe2\x80\x99s property. The court granted summary judgment to Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City, concluding that the FAAAA preempted Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s claims. The New Hampshire Supreme Court reversed. It held the FAAAA\xe2\x80\x99s preemption clause inapplicable because Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s claims related to Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City\xe2\x80\x99s conduct in disposing of his car post-storage, not to conduct concerning \xe2\x80\x9cthe transportation of property,\xe2\x80\x9d or a towing com- pany\xe2\x80\x99s \xe2\x80\x9cservice.\xe2\x80\x9d\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\tHeld:\xc2\xa0Section 14501(c)(1) does not preempt state-law claims stemming from the storage and disposal of a towed vehicle. Pp.\xc2\xa07\xe2\x80\x9313.\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\t(a)\xc2\xa0Where Congress has superseded state legislation by statute, this Court\xe2\x80\x99s task is to \xe2\x80\x9cidentify the domain expressly pre-empted,\xe2\x80\x9d Lorillard Tobacco Co. v. Reilly, \n\n533 U.\xc2\xa0S. 525\n, focusing first on the statutory language, CSX Transp., Inc. v. Easterwood, \n\n507 U.\xc2\xa0S. 658\n. In Rowe v. New Hampshire Motor Transp. Assn., \n\n552 U.\xc2\xa0S. 364\n, this Court\xe2\x80\x99s reading of \xc2\xa714501(c)(1) was informed by decisions interpreting parallel language in the ADA\xe2\x80\x99s preemption clause. Thus, the Court held, the phrase \xe2\x80\x9crelated to\xe2\x80\x9d embraces state laws \xe2\x80\x9chaving a connection with or reference to\xe2\x80\x9d carrier \xe2\x80\x9c\xc2\xa0\xe2\x80\x98rates, routes, or services,\xe2\x80\x99\xc2\xa0\xe2\x80\x9d whether directly or indirectly. Ibid. At the same time, the breadth of the words \xe2\x80\x9crelated to\xe2\x80\x9d does not mean that the preemption clause should be read with an \xe2\x80\x9cuncritical literalism.\xe2\x80\x9d New York State Conference of Blue Cross & Blue Shield Plans v. Travelers Ins. Co., \n\n514 U.\xc2\xa0S. 645\n\xe2\x80\x93656. The Court has cautioned that \xc2\xa714501(c)(1) does not preempt state laws affecting carrier prices, routes, and services \xe2\x80\x9cin only a \xe2\x80\x98tenuous, remote, or peripheral .\xc2\xa0.\xc2\xa0. manner.\xe2\x80\x99\xc2\xa0\xe2\x80\x9d Rowe, 552 U.\xc2\xa0S., at 371. Pp.\xc2\xa07\xe2\x80\x938.\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\t(b)\xc2\xa0Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s state-law claims escape preemption because they are \xe2\x80\x9crelated to\xe2\x80\x9d neither the \xe2\x80\x9ctransportation of property\xe2\x80\x9d nor the \xe2\x80\x9cservice\xe2\x80\x9d of a motor carrier. Although \xc2\xa714501(c)(1) otherwise tracks the ADA\xe2\x80\x99s air-carrier preemption provision, the FAAAA formulation\xe2\x80\x99s one conspicuous alteration\xe2\x80\x94addition of the words \xe2\x80\x9cwith respect to the transportation of property\xe2\x80\x9d\xe2\x80\x94significantly limits the FAAAA\xe2\x80\x99s preemptive scope. It is not sufficient for a state law to relate to the \xe2\x80\x9cprice, route, or service\xe2\x80\x9d of a motor carrier in any capacity; the law must also concern a motor carrier\xe2\x80\x99s \xe2\x80\x9ctransportation of property.\xe2\x80\x9d Title 49 defines \xe2\x80\x9ctransportation,\xe2\x80\x9d in relevant part, as \xe2\x80\x9cservices related to th[e] movement\xe2\x80\x9d of property, \xe2\x80\x9cincluding arranging for .\xc2\xa0.\xc2\xa0. storage [and] handling.\xe2\x80\x9d \xc2\xa713102(23)(B). Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s Consumer Protection Act and negligence claims are not \xe2\x80\x9crelated to th[e] movement\xe2\x80\x9d of his car. Chapter 262 regulates the disposal of vehicles once their transportation\xe2\x80\x94here, by towing\xe2\x80\x94has ended. Pelkey seeks redress only for conduct occurring after the car ceased moving and was stored. Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City maintains that because \xc2\xa713102(23)(B)\xe2\x80\x99s definition of \xe2\x80\x9ctransportation\xe2\x80\x9d includes \xe2\x80\x9cstorage\xe2\x80\x9d and \xe2\x80\x9chandling,\xe2\x80\x9d Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s claims fall within \xc2\xa714501(c)(1)\xe2\x80\x99s preemptive ambit. But \xe2\x80\x9cstorage\xe2\x80\x9d and \xe2\x80\x9chandling\xe2\x80\x9d fit within \xc2\xa713102(23)(B)\xe2\x80\x99s definition only when those services \xe2\x80\x9crelat[e] to th[e] movement\xe2\x80\x9d of property. Thus temporary storage of an item in transit en route to its final destination qualifies as \xe2\x80\x9ctransportation,\xe2\x80\x9d but permanent storage does not. Here, no storage occurred in the course of transporting Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s vehicle.\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tPelkey\xe2\x80\x99s claims are also unrelated to a \xe2\x80\x9cservice\xe2\x80\x9d a motor carrier renders its customers. The transportation service Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City pro-vided\xe2\x80\x94removal of Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s car from his landlord\xe2\x80\x99s parking lot\xe2\x80\x94did involve the movement of property, but that service ended months before the conduct on which Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s claims are based. Because chapter 262, on which Pelkey relies, addresses \xe2\x80\x9cstorage compan[ies]\xe2\x80\x9d and \xe2\x80\x9cgarage owner[s] or keeper[s],\xe2\x80\x9d not transportation activities, it has neither a direct nor an indirect connection to transportation services a motor carrier offers its customers. See Rowe, 552 U.\xc2\xa0S., at 371.\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tThe conclusion that state-law claims regarding disposal of towed vehicles are not preempted is in full accord with Congress\xe2\x80\x99 purpose in enacting \xc2\xa714501(c)(1), which was to displace \xe2\x80\x9ca State\xe2\x80\x99s direct substitution of its own governmental commands for \xe2\x80\x98competitive market forces\xe2\x80\x99 in determining .\xc2\xa0.\xc2\xa0. the services that motor carriers will provide.\xe2\x80\x9d Id., at 372. The New Hampshire prescriptions Pelkey invokes hardly constrain participation in interstate commerce by requiring a motor carrier to offer services not available in the market. Nor do they \xe2\x80\x9cfreez[e] into place services that carriers might prefer to discontinue in the future.\xe2\x80\x9d Ibid. Pp.\xc2\xa08\xe2\x80\x9311.\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\t(c)\xc2\xa0Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City\xe2\x80\x99s additional arguments in favor of preemption are not persuasive. Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City contends that because none of Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s claims fit within the exceptions to preemption detailed in 49 U.\xc2\xa0S.\xc2\xa0C. \xc2\xa7\xc2\xa714501(c)(2), (3), and (5), his claims must be preempted. But exceptions, while sometimes a helpful interpretive guide, do not in themselves delineate the scope of the rule. Here, the exceptions identify matters a State may regulate when it would otherwise be precluded from doing so, but they do not control more than that.\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tDan\xe2\x80\x99s City also maintains that Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s claims are \xe2\x80\x9crelated to\xe2\x80\x9d its towing service because selling Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s car was the means by which Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City obtained payment for the tow. If such state-law claims were preempted, no law would govern resolution of a non-contract-based dispute arising from a towing company\xe2\x80\x99s disposal of a vehicle previously towed or afford a remedy for wrongful disposal. No such design can be attributed to a rational Congress. See Silkwood v. Kerr-McGee Corp., \n\n464 U.\xc2\xa0S. 238\n. Pp.\xc2\xa011\xe2\x80\x9313.\n\t\t\t

\n\t\t\t\tDAN\xe2\x80\x99S CITY USED CARS, INC., dba DAN\xe2\x80\x99S CITY AUTO BODY, PETITIONER v. ROBERT PELKEY\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\ton writ of certiorari to the supreme court of new hampshire\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t[May 13, 2013]\n\t\t\t

\n

\n

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tJustice Ginsburg delivered the opinion of the Court.\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tThis case concerns the preemptive scope of a provision of the Federal Aviation Administration Authorization Act of 1994 (FAAAA or Act) applicable to motor carriers. Codi- fied at \n\n49 U.\xc2\xa0S.\xc2\xa0C. \xc2\xa714501(c)(1), the provision reads:\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\xe2\x80\x9c[A] State . . . may not enact or enforce a law, regulation, or other provision having the force and effect of law related to a price, route, or service of any motor carrier . . . with respect to the transportation of property.\xe2\x80\x9d\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\tPlaintiff-respondent Robert Pelkey brought suit under New Hampshire law against defendant-petitioner Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City Used Cars (Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City), a towing company. Pelkey al- leged that Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City took custody of his car after towing it without Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s knowledge, failed to notify him of its plan to auction the car, held an auction despite Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s communication that he wanted to arrange for the car\xe2\x80\x99s return, and eventually traded the car away without compensating Pelkey for the loss of his vehicle.\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tDisposal of abandoned vehicles by a \xe2\x80\x9cstorage company\xe2\x80\x9d is regulated by chapter 262 of the New Hampshire Revised Statutes Annotated. See N.\xc2\xa0H. Rev. Stat. Ann. \xc2\xa7\xc2\xa7262:31 to 262:40\xe2\x80\x93c (West 2004 and 2012 West Cum. Supp.). Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City relied on those laws to dispose of Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s vehicle for nonpayment of towing and storage fees. According to Pelkey, however, Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City failed to comply with New Hampshire\xe2\x80\x99s provisions governing the sale of stored vehicles and the application of sale proceeds. Pelkey charged that Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City\xe2\x80\x99s disposal of his car without following the requirements contained in chapter 262 violated the New Hampshire Consumer Protection Act, \xc2\xa7358\xe2\x80\x93A:2 (West 2009), as well as Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City\xe2\x80\x99s statutory and common-law duties as bailee to exercise reasonable care while in possession of a bailor\xe2\x80\x99s property.\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tWe hold, in accord with the New Hampshire Supreme Court, that state-law claims stemming from the storage and disposal of a car, once towing has ended, are not sufficiently connected to a motor carrier\xe2\x80\x99s service with respect to the transportation of property to warrant pre- emption under \xc2\xa714501(c)(1). The New Hampshire law in point regulates no towing services, no carriage of prop- erty. Instead, it trains on custodians of stored vehicles seeking to sell them. Congress did not displace the State\xe2\x80\x99s regulation of that activity by any federal prescription.\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\tI\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\tA\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tThe Airline Deregulation Act of 1978 (ADA), \n\n92Stat.\n1705, largely deregulated the domestic airline industry. In keeping with the statute\xe2\x80\x99s aim to achieve \xe2\x80\x9cmaximum reliance on competitive market forces,\xe2\x80\x9d id., at 1706, Congress sought to \xe2\x80\x9censure that the States would not undo federal deregulation with regulation of their own.\xe2\x80\x9d Morales v. Trans World Airlines, Inc., \n\n504 U.\xc2\xa0S. 374, \n378 (1992)\n. Congress therefore included a preemption provision, now codified at \n\n49 U.\xc2\xa0S.\xc2\xa0C. \xc2\xa741713(b)(1), prohibiting States from enacting or enforcing any law \xe2\x80\x9crelated to a price, route, or service of an air carrier.\xe2\x80\x9d\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tTwo years later, the Motor Carrier Act of 1980, 94 Stat. 793, extended deregulation to the trucking industry. Congress completed the deregulation 14 years thereafter, in 1994, by expressly preempting state trucking regulation. Congress did so upon finding that state governance of intrastate transportation of property had become \xe2\x80\x9cunreasonably burden[some]\xe2\x80\x9d to \xe2\x80\x9cfree trade, interstate commerce, and American consumers.\xe2\x80\x9d Columbus v. Ours Ga- rage & Wrecker Service, Inc., \n\n536 U.\xc2\xa0S. 424, \n440 (2002)\n (citing FAAAA \xc2\xa7601(a)(1), \n\n108Stat.\n1605). Borrowing from the ADA\xe2\x80\x99s preemption clause, but adding a new qualification, \xc2\xa7601(c) of the FAAAA supersedes state laws \xe2\x80\x9crelated to a price, route, or service of any motor carrier .\xc2\xa0.\xc2\xa0. with respect to the transportation of property.\xe2\x80\x9d \n\n108Stat.\n1606, now codified at \n\n49 U.\xc2\xa0S.\xc2\xa0C. \xc2\xa714501(c)(1) (emphasis added).\n\t\t\t\t[\n\t\t\t\t\t1\n\t\t\t\t]\n\t\t\t\t The Act exempts certain measures from its preemptive scope, including state laws regulating motor vehicle safety, size, and weight; motor carrier insurance; and the intrastate transportation of household goods. \xc2\xa7\xc2\xa714501(c)(2)(A)\xe2\x80\x93(B). Also exempted from preemption are state laws \xe2\x80\x9crelating to the price\xe2\x80\x9d of \xe2\x80\x9cvehicle transportation by a tow truck,\xe2\x80\x9d if towing occurs without prior consent of the vehicle owner. \xc2\xa714501(c)(2)(C).\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tThis case involves the interaction between the FAAAA\xe2\x80\x99s preemption clause and the State of New Hampshire\xe2\x80\x99s regulation of the removal, storage, and disposal of abandoned motor vehicles. Chapter 262 of the New Hampshire Revised Statutes Annotated establishes procedures by which an \xe2\x80\x9cauthorized official\xe2\x80\x9d or the \xe2\x80\x9cowner . . . of any private property . . . on which a vehicle is parked without permission\xe2\x80\x9d may arrange to have the vehicle towed and stored. N.\xc2\xa0H. Rev. Stat. Ann. \xc2\xa7\xc2\xa7262:31 to 262:34, 262:40\xe2\x80\x93a(I). It generally makes the owner of a towed vehicle responsible for reasonable removal and storage fees. See \xc2\xa7262:33(I) (reasonable removal and storage charges \xe2\x80\x9cshall be a lien against the vehicle which shall be paid by the owner\xe2\x80\x9d); \xc2\xa7262:33(II) (owner entitled to recover vehicle after \xe2\x80\x9cpayment of all reasonable towing and storage charges\xe2\x80\x9d); \xc2\xa7262:40\xe2\x80\x93a(II) (owner of a vehicle towed from a parking lot or parking garage is responsible for \xe2\x80\x9cremoval and storage charges\xe2\x80\x9d when the lot or garage conspicuously posts notice of parking restrictions).\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tUnder chapter 262, the custodian of a car that remains unclaimed for 30 days following a tow may dispose of the vehicle upon compliance with notice requirements. \xc2\xa7\xc2\xa7262:36\xe2\x80\x93a(I), (II). A \xe2\x80\x9cgarage owner or keeper\xe2\x80\x9d must post notices of an impending sale in public places and provide mail notice to the vehicle owner whenever the owner\xe2\x80\x99s address may \xe2\x80\x9cbe ascertained .\xc2\xa0.\xc2\xa0. by the exercise of reasonable diligence.\xe2\x80\x9d \xc2\xa7262:38. If a towed vehicle is not fit for legal use, its custodian need not provide individual or public notice prior to disposal, and sale of the vehicle may occur upon written notice to and approval from New Hampshire\xe2\x80\x99s Department of Public Safety. \xc2\xa7262:36\xe2\x80\x93a(III).\n\t\t\t\t[\n\t\t\t\t\t2\n\t\t\t\t]\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tOn compliance with the statutory requirements, the custodian of a stored vehicle may sell the vehicle by public auction at its place of business. \xc2\xa7262:37. The storage company may use the sale proceeds to pay \xe2\x80\x9cthe amount of the liens and the reasonable expenses incident to the sale.\xe2\x80\x9d \xc2\xa7262:39 (West 2004). Remaining proceeds are payable \xe2\x80\x9cto the [vehicle\xe2\x80\x99s] owner .\xc2\xa0.\xc2\xa0. if claimed at any time within one year from the date of sale.\xe2\x80\x9d Ibid.\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\tB\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tThe landlord of the apartment complex in which Pelkey lived required tenants to remove their cars from the parking lot in the event of a snowstorm, so that the snow could be cleared. Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s 2004 Honda Civic remained in the lot during and after a February 2007 snowstorm. At the landlord\xe2\x80\x99s request, Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City towed and stored the vehicle. Confined to his bed with a serious medical condition, Pelkey did not know his car had been towed. Soon after removal of his car, Pelkey was admitted to the hospital for a procedure to amputate his left foot, during which he suffered a heart attack. He remained under hospital care until his discharge on April 9, 2007.\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tUnaware of Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s identity or illness, Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City sought permission from New Hampshire\xe2\x80\x99s Department of Public Safety to sell the Honda at auction without notice. In response, the department identified Pelkey as the last known owner of the vehicle. Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City wrote to Pelkey, notifying him that it had towed and was storing his car. When the post office returned the letter, checking the box \xe2\x80\x9cmoved, left no address,\xe2\x80\x9d Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City scheduled an auction for April 19. Meanwhile, in the days following Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s discharge from the hospital, his attorney learned from counsel for the apartment complex that the car had been towed by Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City and was scheduled to be sold at pub- lic auction. On April 17, Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s attorney informed Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City that Pelkey wanted to pay any charges owed and reclaim his vehicle. Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City nevertheless proceeded with the auction. Attracting no bidders, Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City later disposed of the car by trading it to a third party. Pelkey was not notified in advance of the trade, and has received no proceeds from the sale.\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tPelkey brought suit against Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City in New Hampshire Superior Court. He alleged that Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City violated the New Hampshire Consumer Protection Act, N.\xc2\xa0H. Rev. Stat. Ann. \xc2\xa7358\xe2\x80\x93A:2, by failing to comply with chapter 262\xe2\x80\x99s requirements for disposal of stored vehicles, mak- ing false statements about the condition and value of his Honda, and proceeding with the auction despite notice that Pelkey wanted to reclaim the car.\n\t\t\t\t[\n\t\t\t\t\t3\n\t\t\t\t]\n\t\t\t\t He also alleged that Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City negligently breached both statutory and common-law duties as a bailee to use reasonable care in disposing of the car. Granting summary judgment to Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City, the New Hampshire Superior Court concluded that Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s claims were preempted by the FAAAA.\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tThe New Hampshire Supreme Court reversed. It held the FAAAA\xe2\x80\x99s preemption clause, \n\n49 U.\xc2\xa0S.\xc2\xa0C. \xc2\xa714501(c)(1), inapplicable because Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s claims related to Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City\xe2\x80\x99s conduct in disposing of his Honda post-storage, not to conduct concerning \xe2\x80\x9cthe transportation of property.\xe2\x80\x9d 163 N.\xc2\xa0H. 483, 490\xe2\x80\x93493, 44 A.\xc2\xa03d 480, 487\xe2\x80\x93489 (2012) (emphasis deleted). Alternatively, the court ruled that, even if Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s claims could be said to concern the transportation of property, they did not \xe2\x80\x9csufficiently relat[e] to a towing company\xe2\x80\x99s \xe2\x80\x98service\xe2\x80\x99 to be preempted.\xe2\x80\x9d Id., at 493, 44 A. 3d, at 490.\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tWe granted certiorari to resolve a division of opinion in state supreme courts on whether \n\n49 U.\xc2\xa0S.\xc2\xa0C. \xc2\xa714501(c)(1) preempts a vehicle owner\xe2\x80\x99s state-law claims against a towing company regarding the company\xe2\x80\x99s post-towing disposal of the vehicle. 568 U.\xc2\xa0S. ___ (2012). Compare 163 N.\xc2\xa0H. 483, 44 A.\xc2\xa03d 480 (this case), with Weatherspoon v. Tillery Body Shop, Inc., 44 So. 3d 447, 458 (Ala. 2010) (\xc2\xa714501(c)(1) preempts state statutory and common-law claims arising out of storage and sale of a towed vehicle).\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\tII\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\tA\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tWhere, as in this case, Congress has superseded state legislation by statute, our task is to \xe2\x80\x9cidentify the domain expressly pre-empted.\xe2\x80\x9d Lorillard Tobacco Co. v. Reilly, 533 U.\xc2\xa0S. 525, 541 (2001). To do so, we focus first on the statutory language, \xe2\x80\x9cwhich necessarily contains the best evidence of Congress\xe2\x80\x99 pre-emptive intent.\xe2\x80\x9d CSX Transp., Inc. v. Easterwood, \n\n507 U.\xc2\xa0S. 658, \n664 (1993)\n.\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tThe FAAAA\xe2\x80\x99s preemption clause prohibits enforcement of state laws \xe2\x80\x9crelated to a price, route, or service of any motor carrier .\xc2\xa0.\xc2\xa0. with respect to the transportation of property.\xe2\x80\x9d \n\n49 U.\xc2\xa0S.\xc2\xa0C. \xc2\xa714501(c)(1). In Rowe v. New Hampshire Motor Transp. Assn., \n\n552 U.\xc2\xa0S. 364, \n370 (2008)\n, our reading of this language was informed by decisions interpreting the parallel language in the ADA\xe2\x80\x99s preemption clause. The phrase \xe2\x80\x9crelated to,\xe2\x80\x9d we said, embraces state laws \xe2\x80\x9chaving a connection with or reference to\xe2\x80\x9d carrier \xe2\x80\x9c\xc2\xa0\xe2\x80\x98rates, routes, or services,\xe2\x80\x99\xc2\xa0\xe2\x80\x9d whether directly or indirectly. Ibid. (quoting Morales, 504 U.\xc2\xa0S., at 384; emphasis deleted). See also id., at 383 (\xe2\x80\x9cordinary meaning of .\xc2\xa0.\xc2\xa0. words [\xe2\x80\x98related to\xe2\x80\x99] is a broad one,\xe2\x80\x9d thus ADA\xe2\x80\x99s use of those words \xe2\x80\x9cexpresses a broad pre-emptive purpose\xe2\x80\x9d).\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tAt the same time, the breadth of the words \xe2\x80\x9crelated to\xe2\x80\x9d does not mean the sky is the limit. We have refused to read the preemption clause of the Employee Retirement Income Security Act of 1974, \n\n29 U.\xc2\xa0S.\xc2\xa0C. \xc2\xa71144(a), which supersedes state laws \xe2\x80\x9crelate[d] to any employee benefit plan,\xe2\x80\x9d with an \xe2\x80\x9cuncritical literalism,\xe2\x80\x9d else \xe2\x80\x9cfor all practical purposes pre-emption would never run its course.\xe2\x80\x9d New York State Conference of Blue Cross & Blue Shield Plans v. Travelers Ins. Co., \n\n514 U.\xc2\xa0S. 645\n\xe2\x80\x93656 (1995) (internal quotation marks omitted). And we have cautioned that \xc2\xa714501(c)(1) does not preempt state laws affecting carrier prices, routes, and services \xe2\x80\x9cin only a \xe2\x80\x98tenuous, remote, or peripheral .\xc2\xa0.\xc2\xa0. manner.\xe2\x80\x99\xc2\xa0\xe2\x80\x9d Rowe, 552 U.\xc2\xa0S., at 371 (quoting Morales, 504 U.\xc2\xa0S., at 390). \n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\tB\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tThe New Hampshire Supreme Court concluded that Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s state-law claims are \xe2\x80\x9crelated to\xe2\x80\x9d neither the \xe2\x80\x9ctransportation of property\xe2\x80\x9d nor the \xe2\x80\x9cservice\xe2\x80\x9d of a motor carrier. We agree.\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tPelkey\xe2\x80\x99s claims escape preemption, we hold, because they are not \xe2\x80\x9crelated to\xe2\x80\x9d the service of a motor carrier \xe2\x80\x9cwith respect to the transportation of property.\xe2\x80\x9d \xc2\xa714501(c)(1). Although \xc2\xa714501(c)(1) otherwise tracks the ADA\xe2\x80\x99s air-carrier preemption provision, see Rowe, 552 U.\xc2\xa0S., at 370, the FAAAA formulation contains one conspicuous alteration\xe2\x80\x94the addition of the words \xe2\x80\x9cwith respect to the transportation of property.\xe2\x80\x9d That phrase \xe2\x80\x9cmassively limits the scope of preemption\xe2\x80\x9d ordered by the FAAAA. Ours Garage, 536 U.\xc2\xa0S., at 449 (Scalia, J., dissenting).\n\t\t\t\t[\n\t\t\t\t\t4\n\t\t\t\t]\n\t\t\t\t As the New Hampshire Supreme Court correctly understood, for purposes of FAAAA preemption, it is not sufficient that a state law relates to the \xe2\x80\x9cprice, route, or service\xe2\x80\x9d of a motor carrier in any capacity; the law must also concern a motor carrier\xe2\x80\x99s \xe2\x80\x9ctransportation of property.\xe2\x80\x9d See 163 N.\xc2\xa0H., at 490, 44 A.\xc2\xa03d, at 487.\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tTitle 49 defines \xe2\x80\x9ctransportation,\xe2\x80\x9d in relevant part, as \xe2\x80\x9cservices related to th[e] movement\xe2\x80\x9d of property, \xe2\x80\x9cincluding arranging for, receipt, delivery, elevation, transfer in transit, refrigeration, icing, ventilation, storage, handling, packing, unpacking, and interchange of passengers and property.\xe2\x80\x9d \xc2\xa713102(23)(B). Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s Consumer Protection Act and negligence claims are not \xe2\x80\x9crelated to th[e] movement\xe2\x80\x9d of his car. Ibid. (emphasis added). He charges Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City with failure to comply with chapter 262 and neglect of its statutory and common-law duties of care as a bailee of his stored vehicle. Chapter 262 does not limit when, where, or how tow trucks may be operated. The Chapter regulates, instead, the disposal of vehicles once their transportation\xe2\x80\x94here, by towing\xe2\x80\x94has ended. Pelkey does not object to the manner in which his car was moved or the price of the tow; he seeks redress only for conduct subsequent to \xe2\x80\x9ctransportation,\xe2\x80\x9d conduct occurring after the car ceased moving and was stored.\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tDan\xe2\x80\x99s City maintains that because \xc2\xa713102(23)(B)\xe2\x80\x99s definition of \xe2\x80\x9ctransportation\xe2\x80\x9d includes \xe2\x80\x9cstorage\xe2\x80\x9d and \xe2\x80\x9chandling,\xe2\x80\x9d Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s claims, which do concern the storage and handling of his car, fall within \xc2\xa714501(c)(1)\xe2\x80\x99s preemp- tive ambit. Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City overlooks, however, that under \xc2\xa713102(23)(B), services such as \xe2\x80\x9cstorage\xe2\x80\x9d and \xe2\x80\x9chandling\xe2\x80\x9d fit within the definition of \xe2\x80\x9ctransportation\xe2\x80\x9d only when those services \xe2\x80\x9crelat[e] to th[e] movement\xe2\x80\x9d of property. Temporary storage of an item in transit en route to its final destination relates to the movement of property and therefore fits within \xc2\xa713102(23)(B)\xe2\x80\x99s definition. But property stored after delivery is no longer in transit. Cf. 49 CFR \xc2\xa7375.609 (2012) (distinguishing between \xe2\x80\x9cstorage-in-transit\xe2\x80\x9d and \xe2\x80\x9cpermanent storage\xe2\x80\x9d (regulation of Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration)). Here, no storage occurred in the course of transporting Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s vehicle. Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City\xe2\x80\x99s storage of Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s car after the towing job was done, in short, does not involve \xe2\x80\x9ctransportation\xe2\x80\x9d within the meaning of the federal Act.\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tPelkey\xe2\x80\x99s claims also survive preemption under \xc2\xa714501(c)(1) because they are unrelated to a \xe2\x80\x9cservice\xe2\x80\x9d a motor carrier renders its customers. The transportation service Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City provided was the removal of Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s car from his landlord\xe2\x80\x99s parking lot. That service, which did involve the movement of property, ended months before the conduct on which Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s claims are based. His claims rely on New Hampshire\xe2\x80\x99s abandoned vehicle disposal regime, prescribed in chapter 262, for the rules governing Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City\xe2\x80\x99s conduct.\n\t\t\t\t[\n\t\t\t\t\t5\n\t\t\t\t]\n\t\t\t\t Chapter 262 addresses \xe2\x80\x9cstorage compan[ies]\xe2\x80\x9d and \xe2\x80\x9cgarage owner[s] or keeper[s],\xe2\x80\x9d not transportation activities. See N.\xc2\xa0H. Rev. Stat. Ann. \xc2\xa7\xc2\xa7262:36\xe2\x80\x93a, 262:38. Unlike Maine\xe2\x80\x99s tobacco delivery regulations at issue in Rowe, chapter 262 has neither a direct nor an indirect connection to any transportation services a motor carrier offers its customers. See 552 U.\xc2\xa0S., at 371. We need not venture an all-purposes definition of transportation \xe2\x80\x9cservice[s]\xe2\x80\x9d in order to conclude that state-law claims homing in on the disposal of stored vehicles fall outside \xc2\xa714501(c)(1)\xe2\x80\x99s preemptive compass.\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tOur conclusion that state-law claims regarding disposal of towed vehicles are not preempted is in full accord with Congress\xe2\x80\x99 purpose in enacting \xc2\xa714501(c)(1). Concerned that state regulation \xe2\x80\x9cimpeded the free flow of trade, traffic, and transportation of interstate commerce,\xe2\x80\x9d Congress resolved to displace \xe2\x80\x9ccertain aspects of the State regulatory process.\xe2\x80\x9d FAAAA \xc2\xa7601(a), \n\n108Stat.\n1605 (emphasis added). The target at which it aimed was \xe2\x80\x9ca State\xe2\x80\x99s direct substitution of its own governmental commands for competitive market forces in determining (to a signifi- cant degree) the services that motor carriers will pro- vide.\xe2\x80\x9d Rowe, 552 U.\xc2\xa0S., at 372 (internal quotation marks omitted).\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tPelkey\xe2\x80\x99s claims are far removed from Congress\xe2\x80\x99 driving concern. He sued under state consumer protection and tort laws to gain compensation for the alleged unlawful disposal of his vehicle. The state laws in question hardly constrain participation in interstate commerce by requiring a motor carrier to offer services not available in the market. Nor do the state laws invoked by Pelkey \xe2\x80\x9cfreez[e] into place services that carriers might prefer to discon- tinue in the future.\xe2\x80\x9d Ibid. New Hampshire\xe2\x80\x99s laws on dis- posal of stored vehicles, moreover, will not open the way for \xe2\x80\x9ca patchwork of state service-determining laws, rules, and regulations.\xe2\x80\x9d Id., at 373. As Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City concedes, abandoned vehicle laws like chapter 262 \xe2\x80\x9cdo not hamper the operations of tow truckers\xe2\x80\x9d and \xe2\x80\x9care not the kind of burdensome state economic regulation Congress sought to preempt.\xe2\x80\x9d Reply Brief 21.\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\tC\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tDan\xe2\x80\x99s City advances two further arguments in favor of preemption. First, Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City contends that Congress\xe2\x80\x99 enumeration of exceptions to preemption, detailed in 49 U.\xc2\xa0S.\xc2\xa0C. \xc2\xa7\xc2\xa714501(c)(2), (3), and (5), permits state regulation of motor carriers only when the State\xe2\x80\x99s law comes within a specified exception. Because Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s claims do not fit within any exception to preemption, Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City urges, those claims must be preempted. This argument exceeds sensible bounds. Exceptions to a general rule, while sometimes a helpful interpretive guide, do not in themselves delineate the scope of the rule. The exceptions to \xc2\xa714501(c)(1)\xe2\x80\x99s general rule of preemption identify matters a State may regulate when it would otherwise be precluded from doing so, but they do not control more than that.\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tAn example may clarify the point. Section 14501(c) does not exempt zoning regulations. Such laws, however, \xe2\x80\x9care peculiarly within the province of state and local legislative authorities.\xe2\x80\x9d Warth v. Seldin, \n\n422 U.\xc2\xa0S. 490\n, n.\xc2\xa018 (1975). It is hardly doubtful that state or local regulation of the physical location of motor-carrier operations falls outside the preemptive sweep of \xc2\xa714501(c)(1). That is so because zoning ordinances ordinarily are not \xe2\x80\x9crelated to a price, route, or service of any motor carrier .\xc2\xa0.\xc2\xa0. with re- spect to the transportation of property.\xe2\x80\x9d \xc2\xa714501(c)(1). The same is true of New Hampshire\xe2\x80\x99s regulation of the dis- posal of stored vehicles.\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tDan\xe2\x80\x99s City, in a second argument, urges otherwise. Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s claims, Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City maintains, are \xe2\x80\x9crelated to\xe2\x80\x9d the towing service it rendered because selling Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s car was the means by which Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City obtained payment for the tow. But if such state-law claims are preempted, no law would govern resolution of a non-contract-based dispute arising from a towing company\xe2\x80\x99s disposal of a vehicle previously towed or afford a remedy for wrongful disposal. Federal law does not speak to these issues.\n\t\t\t\t[\n\t\t\t\t\t6\n\t\t\t\t]\n\t\t\t\t Thus, not only would the preemption urged by Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City leave vehicle owners without any recourse for damages, it would eliminate the sole legal authorization for a towing com- pany\xe2\x80\x99s disposal of stored vehicles that go unclaimed. No such design can be attributed to a rational Congress. See Silkwood v. Kerr-McGee Corp., \n\n464 U.\xc2\xa0S. 238, \n251 (1984)\n (\xe2\x80\x9cIt is difficult to believe that Congress would, without comment, remove all means of judicial recourse for those injured by illegal conduct.\xe2\x80\x9d).\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tIn sum, Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City cannot have it both ways. It cannot rely on New Hampshire\xe2\x80\x99s regulatory framework as authorization for the sale of Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s car, yet argue that Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s claims, invoking the same state-law regime, are preempted. New Hampshire\xe2\x80\x99s legislation on abandoned vehicles gave rise to Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s debt and established the conditions under which Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City could collect on that debt by selling Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s Honda. See N.\xc2\xa0H. Rev. Stat. Ann. \xc2\xa7\xc2\xa7262:33, 262:36\xe2\x80\x93a, 262:40\xe2\x80\x93a; supra, at 3\xe2\x80\x935. Pelkey\xe2\x80\x99s claims, attacking Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City\xe2\x80\x99s conduct in disposing of the vehicle, rest on the very same provisions. See Brief for Petitioner 41 (\xe2\x80\x9cAll of the alleged wrongful conduct of Dan\xe2\x80\x99s City was part of the state sanctioned and regulated process for disposing of abandoned vehicles under Ch. 262.\xe2\x80\x9d).\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t*\xe2\x80\x83\xe2\x80\x83*\xe2\x80\x83\xe2\x80\x83*\n\t\t\t

\n

\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0\n\t\t\t\tFor the reasons stated, we hold that \n\n49 U.\xc2\xa0S.\xc2\xa0C. \xc2\xa714501(c)(1) does not preempt state-law claims for dam- ages stemming from the storage and disposal of a towed vehicle. The judgment of the New Hampshire Supreme Court is therefore affirmed.\n\t\t\t
