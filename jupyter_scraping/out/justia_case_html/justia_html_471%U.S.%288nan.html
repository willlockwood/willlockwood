\n

PER CURIAM.

\n

On April 21, 1981, respondent commenced this action alleging, in part, that the petitioner School District discriminated

Page 471 U. S. 289

against her on the basis of sex in failing to promote her to an administrative position. She sought equitable and compensatory relief under 42 U.S.C. \xc2\xa7 1983 for the alleged acts of discrimination which occurred in August, 1979, May, 1980, and September, 1980.

\n

The District Court dismissed the \xc2\xa7 1983 claim because it was not brought within the 6-month limitations period which applies to

\n

"[a]n action against any officer of any government unit for anything done in the execution of his office, except an action subject to another limitation specified in this subchapter."

\n

42 Pa.Cons.Stat. \xc2\xa7 5522(b)(1) (1982). The Court of Appeals reversed, holding that the "application of the six-month limitations period would be inconsistent with the policies and legislative history underlying \xc2\xa7 1983," and that "the six-year residuary provision of the limitations scheme should govern this dispute." 699 F.2d 137, 139 (CA3 1983). We granted certiorari, 468 U.S. 1204 (1984), and heard argument.

\n

The judgment of the Court of Appeals is now vacated, and the case is remanded for further consideration in light of our decision in Wilson v. Garcia, ante p. 471 U. S. 261, in which we have held that all \xc2\xa7 1983 claims should be characterized for statute of limitations purposes as actions to recover damages for injuries to the person.

\n

It is so ordered.

\n

JUSTICE POWELL took no part in the consideration or decision of this case.
